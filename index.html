How WaveKit works

Suggestions with context, not pressure.

WaveKit looks at the Resonators and weapons you mark as owned, then builds teams around known archetypes, role coverage, safety, and realistic substitutions.

Local Your profiles stay in this browser.
01

Account-aware

Suggestions come from what you actually own, not a perfect account that expects every premium unit.

02

Archetype-first

Teams are weighted by mechanics like Aero Erosion, Spectro Frazzle, Echo Skill, Heavy Attack, and Tune shells.

03

Comfort-aware

Real healers and defensive picks are treated differently from utility supports, so risky teams are easier to spot.

04

Honest data

Newer or less-confirmed character data is marked for review instead of being presented as absolute truth.

Quick answers

Wuthering Waves teams without the pressure.

WaveKit is built for players searching for a WuWa team builder, Wuthering Waves team helper, beginner team advice, or a practical way to check which Resonators can work together.

I own these characters. What team should I use?

Mark your Resonators and weapons, then compare practical Wuthering Waves team suggestions using owned characters instead of only perfect premium examples.

Which teams are safer for beginners?

WaveKit separates real healers, defensive supports, fallback fits, and stronger archetype teams so newer players can avoid stressful recommendations.

What weapons, Echoes, and Sonata sets should I build?

Character pages keep build direction readable, with best weapons, alternate weapons, Echo cost notes, Sonata effects, and team role context.

Data notes

Built to be honest about certainty.

WaveKit is a fan-made helper shaped by released-character data, public theorycrafting, and player reports. Team scoring is conservative: it prefers proven shells and sensible alternatives over risky guesses.

Phone and desktop install

Add WaveKit like an app.

WaveKit includes web-app support for installable browsers. On iPhone and iPad, Safari is the most reliable Home Screen path. On supported Android and desktop Chrome browsers, WaveKit can be installed from the browser menu or install prompt.

App icon Uses the WaveKit logo you already see on the site.
WaveKit Installable web app
  1. iPhone or iPadOpen WaveKit in Safari, tap Share, then choose Add to Home Screen.
  2. Android ChromeOpen WaveKit in Chrome, then choose Install app or Add to Home screen if Chrome shows the option.
  3. Desktop ChromeUse the install icon in the address bar, or open the browser menu and choose install when available.
  4. Open from the iconWaveKit launches with its own icon and keeps your local profile on that device.
