New player reading guide

Aemeath's modes are separate team plans. Denia belongs to Fusion Burst, Lynae belongs to Tune Rupture, and Lupa enables Mono Fusion. WaveKit will not mix those specialist partners into an incompatible team.

Mode is set manually, not automatic: before the fight, open Aemeath's own character details screen (not the team-building screen) and choose Tune Rupture or Fusion Burst. Her mode-specific partner needs to be set to the same mode too, or the synergy will not trigger.

How the kit can be played

Playstyles and modes

Resonance Mode - Tune Rupture

When in Resonance Mode - Tune Rupture, Aemeath can inflict Tune Rupture - Shifting on the target, and Seraphic Duet can deal additional Tune Rupture DMG.

Resonance Mode - Fusion Burst

When in Resonance Mode - Fusion Burst, the required number of stacks to trigger Fusion Burst reduces. Aemeath can inflict Fusion Burst on the target, and Seraphic Duet can trigger Fusion Burst based on its max stack limit without removing its stacks.

Notable milestones

Aemeath's chain levels mostly add power spikes and comfort rather than changing her core plan. R2, R3, and R6 are the largest direct upgrades; R1, R4, and R5 add safety and utility.

Aemeath FAQ

What is the best Aemeath build in Wuthering Waves?

Aemeath's recommended WaveKit setup is Tune Rupture/Fusion Burst DPS Build, using Everbright Polestar, Trailblazing Star, and Sigillum as the main Echo target.

What stats should Aemeath use?

Start with Crit Rate or Crit DMG / Fusion DMG or ATK% / ATK%. If the rotation feels uncomfortable, improve Energy Regen or defensive comfort before chasing perfect substats.

What teams work with Aemeath?

Use Aemeath / Denia / Chisa for Fusion Burst, Aemeath / Lynae / Mornye for Tune Rupture, or Aemeath / Lupa / Mornye for Mono Fusion. These mode-specific partners should not be mixed indiscriminately.

Is Aemeath beginner friendly?

Aemeath's current difficulty is listed as Moderate. If you are learning, pair them with safer sustain or defensive support.
